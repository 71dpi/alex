Leading Kroger's Foundational Product Design Team Through Transformation

TL;DR
Managed Kroger's Foundational Design Team through a strategic expansion—shifting from systems architecture to growth-focused point solutions. Built a hybrid, multidisciplinary team that improved personalization, trust, search relevance, and assortment availability while preserving revenue drivers and scaling design delivery.

Overview

As manager of Kroger's Foundational Design Team, I led a multidisciplinary, hybrid group—design architects, user researchers, UX and product designers, plus remote and contractor contributors. This structure allowed us to create system-level foundations while delivering point solutions that drove measurable growth.

Why “Foundational Design”?

The team owned core domains that form the backbone of digital commerce: assortment & strategy, trust & privacy, personalization, and search. These capabilities were essential to reliably display products and enable customers to shop across touchpoints.

  • Assortment & Strategy: Ensuring the right products and categories are available and discoverable.
  • Trust & Privacy: Building confidence in data security and transparency.
  • Personalization: Delivering tailored experiences to drive engagement and conversion.
  • Search: Powering product discovery and navigation—the primary entry point for many shoppers.

Team Composition & Hybrid Model

Our team combined system thinkers and execution-focused designers:

  • Design Architects: Shaped system frameworks and design principles.
  • User Researchers: Drove insights via usability testing and JTBD mapping.
  • UX & Product Designers: Translated strategy into scalable experiences.

The hybrid model enabled global talent leverage, remote agility, and in-person collaboration for high-impact work.

Strategic Expansion

We expanded from foundational architecture to point solutions that directly influenced product roadmaps and growth metrics—improving personalization, trust, and availability, and lifting add-to-cart (ATC) and engagement through search and curated pathways.

Key Initiatives

1. Foundational Change

Reimagined patterns and layouts across Home, Search, and Browse; prototyped conversational AI search; and built lighthouse prototypes combining trust, personalization, and assortment filtering.

2. Agentic Shopping

Led agentic commerce explorations—differentiating conversational AI from embedded agent flows—enabling automated cart-building (dietary/budget constraints) and integrating real-time inventory and loyalty personalization.

3. Full App Redesign

Contributed to Kroger's native app refresh: a food-first experience, simplified navigation, centralized savings & health integrations, and a scalable design system (Citrus/KDS 2.0) for component-based experiences.

Maintaining Revenue Drivers

While evolving the product, we safeguarded major revenue channels by optimizing search relevance and running personalization experiments that improved ATC and engagement.

Impact

  • Delivered measurable CX and engagement improvements.
  • Positioned Kroger as an early leader in agentic shopping and omnichannel design.
  • Built a resilient, high-performing team capable of executing at scale and adapting to new technologies.
