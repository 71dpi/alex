Kroger App Redesign IA & Navigation

Role: Information Architect & Lead Designer • Platforms: iOS & Android • Timeline: 2025 Digital Refresh Initiative

TL;DR

Led IA and native design for Kroger's 2025 app refresh, simplifying navigation, centralizing savings, and delivering a food-first, accessible experience. The redesign unified experiences across iOS and Android, improved discovery and savings utilization, and supported rapid adoption during rollout.

Background & Opportunity

Kroger's digital experience had become function-first and fragmented. Competitors were investing in richer, more personal experiences, and customer feedback highlighted navigation and savings discovery gaps. The refresh aimed to deliver a unified, intuitive, and rewarding app across shopping missions.

Goals, Mission & Vision

Mission: Grow trust by giving customers personalized ways to shop for what they want, when and how they need it—without compromise.

Vision: Make every digital interaction personal and essential—guided by preferences, powered by technology, and designed to feel effortless.

Experience Aspiration: Grocery shopping should feel personal and essential—guided by taste, supported by intentional technology, and designed to make every visit effortless and uniquely yours.

Principles: Personalized like a trusted shopkeeper; connect and uplift with warm, conversational language; supportive, not just smart.

Strategic Objectives

  • Simplify navigation for shopping, savings, and health services.
  • Centralize savings so offers are discoverable and actionable.
  • Emphasize food-first storytelling with clearer imagery and less clutter.
  • Improve accessibility and platform performance for iOS and Android.

Scope & Mandatories

Full native redesign on iOS and Android using Citrus Design System. Mandatory areas: accessibility, analytics, and comprehensive testing. Workstreams spanned Virtual Assistant, Assortment Display, Rx Mixed Basket, Fresh, Shop, Savings, Health, Meals, and Search.

Role & Contributions

  • IA & Content Strategy: Defined navigation, content hierarchy, and interaction patterns across app surfaces.
  • Interaction Design & Native Prototypes: Partnered with iOS and Android engineers to build and validate native-forward prototypes.
  • Design System Integration: Applied design system standards to ensure scalable, reusable components.
  • Cross-Functional Leadership: Aligned UI designers, content strategists, analytics, and engineering for feasible delivery.

Customer-Centric Framework

JTBD-guided design decisions focused on core customer jobs:

  • Shopping however I want—frictionless across in-store, online, and delivery.
  • Finding what fits me best—personalized recommendations and filters.
  • Making healthy food habits easy—integrated wellness tools.
  • Enabling last-minute meals—quick, convenient solutions.

Progress & Outcomes

Refresh Delivered: September 2025.

Impact: Unified experience solved navigation and savings pain points, improved discovery and savings utilization, and positioned Kroger to regain market share.

Next Steps

  • Continue evolving personalization and AI-driven features post-MVP.
  • Expand dynamic components for seasonal and contextual experiences.
  • Monitor KPIs for engagement, repeat rate, and savings utilization.
