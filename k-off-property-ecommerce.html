Driving Off-Property Ecommerce Through Design Strategy

TL;DR

Led design strategy for Kroger's off-property ecommerce initiative—creating a scalable playbook and play cards to standardize listings, reduce turnaround, and maintain brand integrity across partners like DoorDash, Instacart, and Uber Eats. The approach improved operational efficiency, increased engagement, and enabled rapid partner rollouts.

Background & Context

As customers migrated to multi-channel shopping, Kroger aimed to meet them on retail aggregators (DoorDash, Uber Eats, Instacart). Off-property processes were fragmented, producing inconsistent customer experiences and operational inefficiencies. A unified playbook was needed to scale execution while preserving brand and commerce quality.

Mission & Goals

Mission: Build a comprehensive off-property ecommerce strategy and playbook that delivers consistent, high-quality experiences across third-party models while driving measurable business outcomes.

  • Expand Kroger's digital footprint through off-property models.
  • Meet customers where they are by optimizing experiences on retail aggregators.
  • Ensure brand consistency and compliance across partner ecosystems.
  • Improve operational workflows for faster execution and fewer errors.
  • Enhance customer experience with optimized design and usability.

Objectives

  • Develop a design framework that supports scalability and adaptability across multiple models.
  • Create standardized templates and guidelines for product listings, imagery, and promotions.
  • Align design decisions with business and technical requirements through cross-functional collaboration.
  • Integrate KPIs into the playbook to track adoption and performance.

Role & Contributions

As Senior Product Designer I led the design strategy and execution for the playbook. Key activities included:

  • Audited existing off-property experiences to identify gaps and inconsistencies.
  • Designed modular, reusable templates that balanced brand integrity with partner specs.
  • Facilitated stakeholder workshops to align on principles and usability standards.
  • Partnered with product managers and engineers to enable seamless implementation.
  • Organized teams to operationalize the playbook through model-specific play cards.

Role & Contributions

  • Consistency: Unified visual language across off-property models.
  • Efficiency: Reduced time-to-market via reusable templates and clear workflows.
  • Scalability: Flexible framework accommodating new partnerships and requirements.
  • Customer Experience: Improved clarity and usability in listings, increasing engagement.

Key Unlocks

  • APIs and frictionless checkout for seamless integration and reduced customer friction.
  • AI and connected devices identified as opportunities for future personalization and automation.

Process Design, Measurement & Staffing

We defined inbound/outbound workflows, established KPIs (adoption, engagement, operational efficiency), and created staffing models to handle partner-driven requests and Kroger-led initiatives—ensuring agility and scale.

Metrics & Outcomes

  • Adoption: 100% implementation of the playbook across identified off-property models within the first quarter.
  • Operational Efficiency: 30% reduction in design and content turnaround time.
  • Engagement: 15% increase in click-through rates on off-property listings.
  • Stakeholder Feedback: Positive responses citing improved clarity and ease of execution.

Lessons Learned & Future Outlook

  • Early cross-functional collaboration accelerates alignment and execution.
  • Scalable design systems are essential for managing multi-model complexity.
  • Continuous iteration using performance data sustains success.

Next: evolve the playbook with AI-driven personalization, integrate connected devices, and adapt to emerging partner capabilities.
