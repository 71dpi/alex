Case Study: Gamification at Kroger

Background

Kroger explored gamification as a strategy to enhance customer engagement and drive incremental revenue through multi-step offers (MSOs). Inspired by popular experiences like Animal Crossing, Starbucks Rewards, and Fortnite, the initiative aimed to make grocery shopping more interactive and rewarding.

Challenges Identified

Poor Findability and Usability of Multi-Step Offers

Research revealed that customers struggled to locate and understand MSOs. The complexity of navigating bonus rewards and unclear UI patterns led to low engagement and frustration. Key usability tests highlighted that even active rewards were not easily discoverable, reducing the perceived value of participation. [CX - Gamif...8_14-49-32 | PDF]

Absence of a Long-Term Loyalty Strategy

While gamification provided short-term excitement, it lacked alignment with Kroger’s broader loyalty objectives. There was no cohesive plan to transition gamified experiences into sustained customer retention, leaving a gap in strategic continuity. [Gamificati...8_18-31-23 | PDF]

Design and Research Interventions

Prototyping and Iterative Testing

The design team conducted rapid prototyping and usability studies to improve MSO interfaces. These efforts temporarily boosted engagement and conversion rates for existing offers, validating that better UX could mitigate some friction. [CX - Gamif...8_14-49-32 | PDF]

Customer Sentiment Analysis

Surveys and qualitative feedback revealed a critical insight: gamification in grocery shopping often made customers feel devalued and off-put. Instead of fostering loyalty, the experience was perceived as gimmicky, undermining Kroger’s brand promise of simplicity and trust. [Gamificati...8_18-31-23 | PDF]

Business Impact

Revenue and Spend Behavior

Data showed that while gamification shifted spend forward or backward, it did not generate incremental revenue. Customers were “playing the system” without increasing overall basket size or frequency. This misalignment with financial goals reinforced the need to pivot away from gamification. [CX - Gamif...8_14-49-32 | PDF]

Strategic Recommendation

As Senior Product Designer, I recommended abandoning the roadmap for expanding MSOs through gamification. Instead, the focus should shift to loyalty-driven initiatives such as:

  • Membership programs
  • Rewards optimization
  • Personalized couponing

These approaches align with customer expectations and Kroger’s business model, fostering genuine loyalty rather than short-lived engagement spikes.

Key Takeaways

  • Gamification was fun to explore but ultimately incompatible with Kroger’s value proposition.
  • Long-term loyalty strategies outperform gamified mechanics in grocery retail.
  • Customer trust and perceived value are paramount—complex games erode both.
