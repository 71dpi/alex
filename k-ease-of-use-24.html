Kroger Ease-of-Use Enhancements

TL;DR

In 2024 Kroger’s Experience Optimization effort improved ease-of-use scores and overall customer satisfaction by simplifying navigation, improving discoverability, and introducing consistent UI components—resulting in higher engagement and measured gains in cart adds and conversion on targeted tests.

Overview

In 2024, Kroger launched a major Experience Optimization effort aimed at improving ease-of-use benchmarking scores and overall customer satisfaction. The initiative focused on findability and usability, ensuring customers could shop effortlessly across the Kroger website and app. Work was grounded in design thinking workshops, rigorous research, and iterative testing, leading to navigation and UI enhancements.

Goals

  • Improve Kroger’s “Easy to Shop” equity ranking and overall digital experience score.
  • Reduce friction in navigation and task completion.
  • Increase engagement, conversion, and revenue through better usability and discoverability.

Research & Methods

The team used a comprehensive set of UX research and testing methods, including:

  • Design thinking workshops to align cross-functional teams and ideate solutions.
  • Usability testing and A/B experiments (UserZoom and live A/B tests) for iterative validation.
  • Card sorts and tree testing to validate navigation architecture.
  • Surveys and customer panels to capture feedback on navigation clarity and ease of use.

Key Design Changes

Navigation Redesign

Unified main and secondary navigation across web and native platforms. Introduced Quick Links for utility navigation and a Discovery Marquee for promotional content to improve discoverability and reduce task time.

New Components

Introduced a Mission Header template combining App Bar, Quick Links, and Discovery Marquee for consistency, plus Story Blocks for promotional storytelling and inspiration.

Content Enhancements

Improved visual design for clarity and consistency and added storytelling elements to drive engagement and inspiration.

Impact on Metrics

Engagement

Navigation A/B tests showed increased clicks and engagement for Quick Links and Media Link treatments without harming overall conversion.

Conversion & Revenue

Story blocks and personalized carousels increased cart adds and produced incremental revenue gains on targeted tests, while checkout conversion was maintained or improved in experiments.

Customer Sentiment

Composite satisfaction scores improved; ease-of-use was among the strongest drivers of the uplift in digital satisfaction.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

The initiative was led by a senior product designer who conducted much of the research and prototyping, partnering with DX, AMP, Core, and engineering teams. Alignment was maintained via weekly reviews and governance processes.

Results & Learnings

  • Simplification and consistency were key drivers of improved usability.
  • Customers responded positively to clearer navigation and personalized content.
  • Future focus areas: adaptive IA, AI-driven personalization, and predictive shopping experiences.
